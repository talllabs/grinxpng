Research & Discovery

Before building a brand,
we must understand the full picture

Our research methodology is designed to build a comprehensive, evidence-based foundation for the Papua New Guinea destination brand. We approach this systematically — from quantitative data gathering to qualitative community voices — ensuring no stone is left unturned.

The methodology includes desk research, primary research with key audiences, and a structured synthesis process that feeds directly into brand strategy development.

  • Desk research and secondary data review
  • Quantitative survey design and deployment
  • Qualitative interview frameworks
  • Data synthesis and insight mapping
  • Brand audit against current positioning
  • Gap analysis and opportunity identification

Understanding how Papua New Guinea is currently perceived by international travellers — and non-travellers — is critical. We will study awareness, associations, and emotional connections with the destination across priority markets.

This research identifies both barriers to visitation and compelling motivators that can anchor the new brand positioning.

  • International traveller perception surveys
  • Awareness and consideration tracking in priority markets
  • Emotional association mapping
  • Barrier and motivator analysis
  • Comparison with competing destination perceptions

A thorough review of media coverage, travel editorial, social media, review platforms and digital content will reveal how Papua New Guinea is currently being discussed, portrayed and shared by travellers, journalists and influencers.

Sentiment analysis helps identify narrative gaps and brand opportunities in the existing media ecosystem.

  • Media coverage audit (print and digital)
  • Social media sentiment analysis
  • Review platform analysis (TripAdvisor, Google, etc.)
  • Travel blogger and influencer content review
  • Search trend analysis by market
  • Earned media narrative mapping

Broad stakeholder consultation is essential to ensure the new brand genuinely reflects the people and communities of Papua New Guinea. We will engage government, industry operators, community leaders, and tourism partners throughout the process.

These consultations are not box-ticking — they are integral to developing a brand that has authentic buy-in from those who live it every day.

  • PNGTPA leadership and government stakeholder interviews
  • Tourism operator workshops across regions
  • Community and cultural leader consultations
  • International trade and travel agent input
  • Airlines and key industry partner briefings
  • Online consultation platforms for broader reach

Understanding how competitor destinations position themselves helps identify white space where Papua New Guinea can claim a genuinely differentiated and ownable position. We assess both regional and global competitors.

This analysis reveals conventions to challenge and opportunities to lead — ensuring PNG isn't following a category playbook but setting its own.

  • Regional competitor brand audit (Pacific, Southeast Asia)
  • Global adventure and cultural destination benchmarking
  • Positioning statement analysis
  • Visual identity and tone of voice comparison
  • Campaign and messaging review
  • Market differentiation mapping

Brand ideas will be tested with real audiences before being finalised. This ensures the new destination brand resonates as intended — in priority markets and across the diverse communities of Papua New Guinea.

Testing is iterative, allowing us to refine ideas based on real feedback rather than assumptions, and giving PNGTPA confidence before committing to final creative direction.

  • Concept testing with target traveller segments
  • Positioning statement testing in priority markets
  • Visual identity concept evaluation
  • Message testing with industry stakeholders
  • Iterative refinement based on testing feedback
  • Final validation before brand launch
